Biography of Johnny W. Carson

Early Years

Born in Corning, Iowa on October 23, 1925 to Ruth and Homer R. Carson, a power company manager, Johnny Carson learned how to reel in audiences at a young age.

Following high school, in 1943, an 18-year-old Carson joined the U.S. Navy as an ensign, and then decoded encrypted messages as a communications officer. Though assigned to combat in the summer of 1945, Carson never went into battle.

In the fall of 1945, Carson began studying at the University of Nebraska, and received a bachelor's degree in radio and speech four years later. After college, he had a short stint as television writer for The Red Skelton Show in Los Angeles, and then moved to New York City in pursuit of bigger audiences.

The Tonight Show

In October of 1962, Carson replaced Jack Paar as host of The Tonight Show-a counterpart to NBC's Today show-and, following wavering ratings his first year, Carson became a prime-time hit.

Carson was The Tonight Show's host for three decades. During that time, he received six Emmy Awards, a Peabody Award and the Presidential Medal of Freedom. Carson's final appearance as host in 1992 attracted an estimated 50 million viewers.

Death

In January of 2005, at age 79, Carson died of respiratory failure caused by emphysema.
